India could break up if corruption not tackled
BY SUBHAM GOYAL
27 February 2014
India would not survive - and millions of Indians would remain poor and illiterate if it doesn't tackle corruption in its political system, the leader of its newest party has warned.

Arvind Kejriwal of the anti-corruption Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) and Party's member will fight the forthcoming general elections.

Former Delhi chief minister Arvind Kejriwal spent 49 days in power. Mr Kejriwal resigned after an anti-corruption bill was blocked in the Delhi assembly. He said it was corrupt politicians who were ultimately responsible for the country's regular outbreaks of communal violence.

"Indians are first class people suffering third class governance. If you go abroad, to America or to London, you will see Indians shining in their areas. But in their own country they are not allowed to prosper,"

- Arvind Kejriwal

"My job is to change the governance system so India can shine."

Mr Kejriwal didn't want to be prime minister, but he declared himself an "anarchist on a mission to shake up the Indian system".

Arvind Kejriwal takes Lok Sabha battle to Narendra Modi's home turf

Arvind Kejriwal is seeking to attack Modi primarily on two issues – corruption and communalism. It seems , AAP has decided to take on BJP's prime ministerial candidate Narendra Modi head on.

AAP chief and former Delhi chief minister – will travel to Gujarat likely on March 8 and 9 and hold road shows ahead of the General Elections due by May.

The AAP has decided to take the Lok Sabha battle right into Modi's home turf following the realisation that its direct contest will be against the BJP and not the Congress, which is on a down-slide nationwide.

Various opinion polls have predicted that the Congress party may not even cross the 100-seat mark in the impending Lok Sabha polls. The BJP, on the other hand, is likely to win over 200 seats.

Kejriwal has been hitting out at Modi over funding of his rallies and helicopter rides. He also accused him of benefitting gas companies and being under the influence of Reliance chief Mukesh Ambani.

"From where does Modi get so much money for his rallies?
Who is paying for his helicopters?"
